How to create a safe environment and experience for your children online

After we listed the majority of the problems that face children daily while using the internet, we’re now focusing on how you can make sure that your child is safe every time he opens his device or browser. There are two main parts that go into creating a safe online environment and healthy teach using manners by your children.

  • Technical steps including software, and parental control settings
  • Our approach in dealing and using the internet

Parental control software and filtering software

  • Google safe search

    The easiest thing you can do is to turn on Safe Search mode in google which instantly filters all the explicit search results.
  • internet filtering software

    Qustodio is an internet filtering software that filters unwanted content and allow you to see and manage your child’s activity, it creates and send reports daily, weekly or monthly by your request. It works with android, it works on all devices windows, mac , android and iOS.
  • Apple parental controls

    For apple users you can also block inappropriate content using user parental controls, on macs, iPhones and iPads. You can also control lots of other features and settings like preventing purchases on iTunes and the app store and many other important features. Here’s a full guide made by apple for you that will help in every step.
  • creating a google account for your child

    You can create an account for your child that will be linked to your account, where you can manage it by filtering content, managing his/her screen time and choosing what apps they can download all by using the family link app.

Our approach in dealing with and using the internet

What we have to understand that software might fail, settings might be edited and nothing Is 100% safe. The most important thing you have to do is change the way your children use the internet and talk to them more. Here are some steps that might help:

  1. Communication

    The most important thing I might say that you keep an open line of conversation between you and your children, make them feel safe, so whenever they see anything disturbing or inappropriate or anything that annoys them, they will come to you first.
  2. Inform

    Talk with your children at what they might see online and how they can deal with it.
  3. Guidelines and restrictions

    Set healthy reasonable boundaries on what information they can share online and how much time should they be spending online.
  4. Online friends

    Learn if they have any online friends and make sure that you know about them.
  5. Adapt

    Adapt to the new technology they’re using, and ask your children for help if needed, no one knows how to use them better.
  6. Delay handheld devices

    Delay devices like phones, tablets and iPads until the age of 13 at least, instead provide devices like laptops and computers and place them in public rooms.
  7. If it’s too good to be true it probably isn’ t

    Teach your kids to stick to this mantra when it comes to ads and online shopping.
